SPIDER-MAN

ABOUT SPIDERMAN

Spider-Man is a famous superhero from Marvel Comics, created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko. His real name is Peter Parker, a young student living in New York City. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, he gains incredible abilities like super strength, the power to stick to walls, and a special “spider-sense” that helps him detect danger. Despite having amazing powers, Spider-Man faces many real-life struggles such as managing school, work, and personal relationships. This makes him one of the most relatable superheroes. He follows the important lesson, “With great power comes great responsibility,” which guides him to use his powers to protect people and fight villains.

Spider-Man: No Way Home

Spider-Man: No Way Home is a popular superhero movie starring Tom Holland as Peter Parker. In this film, Spider-Man’s identity is revealed to the world, which creates serious problems in his personal life. To fix this, he seeks help from Doctor Strange, but a magical spell goes wrong and opens the multiverse. Because of this, villains from other universes like Green Goblin and Doctor Octopus enter his world. Spider-Man must face these powerful enemies while also dealing with emotional challenges and sacrifices. The movie is loved for its action, emotional story, and surprising appearances, making it one of the most successful Spider-Man films of all time.

The Amazing Spider-Man

The Amazing Spider-Man is a superhero movie starring Andrew Garfield as Peter Parker. This film shows a different version of Spider-Man’s origin story, focusing more on his parents’ mystery and his journey to discover the truth about his past. After being bitten by a genetically altered spider, Peter gains superpowers and begins to understand his responsibilities. In the movie, he faces the dangerous villain Lizard, who was once a scientist named Dr. Curt Connors. Alongside action and adventure, the film also highlights Peter’s relationship with Gwen Stacy. The movie is known for its emotional depth, modern visuals, and a more realistic portrayal of Spider-Man’s struggles and life.
